The Xplorer's frame is built entirely from 30×30 mm aluminum extrusion profiles, providing a strong and stable foundation for quality printing.

The assembly process is intentionally straightforward, with a simple and logical structure that's easy to follow—even for first-time builders.

To ensure dimensional accuracy and perfect alignment, milled reference surfaces are used in all critical areas, ensuring that components align precisely during assembly.
The result is a frame that remains square, rigid, and resistant to vibration, even during high-speed or multi-toolhead operations.

## Frame Assembly Process

This chapter guides you through the complete frame assembly in six main sections:

**Chapter 2.1: Frame Beam MGN Y-Axis** - We begin by assembling the central Y-axis frame beam, installing the MGN12 linear rail with precision alignment jigs to ensure perfect rail positioning and smooth motion.

**Chapter 2.2: Front Pillar** - Next, we construct and install the front vertical pillars, creating the structural support for the front of the printer and establishing the proper frame geometry.

**Chapter 2.3: Frame Left Side** - We then assemble the complete left side frame structure, including vertical supports and horizontal cross-members, ensuring proper alignment with the main frame.

**Chapter 2.4: Frame Right Side** - Following the left side assembly, we construct the right side frame with identical procedures, maintaining perfect symmetry and structural integrity.

**Chapter 2.5: Frame Backside** - We complete the main frame structure by assembling the backside components, including upper and lower aluminium profiles that tie the entire frame together.

**Chapter 2.6: Frame Completion** - In the final section, we integrate all frame assemblies, install the remaining aluminium profiles, add rubber feet, and perform quality inspection to ensure the frame meets all specifications.

Throughout this assembly process, we use standard tools like Allen keys, alignment jigs, and systematic verification to ensure every component is positioned correctly and securely fastened, resulting in a professional-quality frame ready for the next stages of printer assembly.

This solid foundation is crucial to the Xplorer's modular architecture and its ability to deliver consistently reliable print quality.
